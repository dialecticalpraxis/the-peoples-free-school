_How did this come to be? What were the conditions, struggles, and contingencies that produced the current arrangement?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Historical|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes.

This is the final part of the socialism series, and it follows the revolution out of Europe and into the colonized and formerly colonized world, where socialism and national liberation fused. The stories here run from Cuba and Allende's Chile through Sandinista Nicaragua and Evo Morales's Bolivia, and across Africa from Sankara's Burkina Faso to Nyerere's Tanzania and Nkrumah's Ghana, to the brief revolution in Grenada. They are stories of extraordinary hope and, often, of siege, coup, and implosion, and this lens tells them from more than one side. It builds on the national-liberation histories in [[03-themes/imperialism-and-the-global-system/index|Imperialism and the Global System]] and continues the series from [[03-themes/revolution-in-power/index|Revolution in Power]].

## Anchor questions

- What did these revolutions promise the poor of the Global South, and where did they deliver?
- How much of each story is internal, and how much is coup, blockade, and the long arm of empire?
- Grenada's revolution destroyed itself before the US invaded. Nicaragua's turned authoritarian. What do the implosions teach?
- Why do we know Che and Castro but not Sankara, Cabral, or Bishop?
- Whose telling of these histories have we inherited, and who wrote it?

## Materials

Free unless marked otherwise. "Free to borrow" means a free Internet Archive account and a renewable loan. Standpoints are noted; the sources are meant to be read against one another.

### Cuba, Chile, and Venezuela

- **The Cuban Revolution** (The Cold War, 2022, 19 min). A mainstream video baseline on Batista's fall and Castro's rise. Free: [youtube.com/watch?v=kDeKySs_nxw](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kDeKySs_nxw)
- **Peter Winn** (b. 1942), _Weavers of Revolution_ (1986). Chilean workers who pushed Allende further than he wished to go, the peaceful road against worker self-activity. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/weaversofrevolut00pete)
- **George Ciccariello-Maher**, _We Created Chávez_ (2013). The Bolivarian process as decades of grassroots movement-building, not one leader's project. Not free; [publisher page](https://www.dukeupress.edu/we-created-chavez).

### Central and South America

- **Matilde Zimmermann**, _Sandinista: Carlos Fonseca and the Nicaraguan Revolution_ (2000). How the Sandinista revolution was made, from 1961 to 1979. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/sandinistacarlos0000zimm_o4k5)
- **Mateo Jarquin**, "Reckoning with Revolution in Nicaragua" (2020). The line from 1979 to Ortega's authoritarian turn, without romance or demonization. Free: [ageofrevolutions.com](https://ageofrevolutions.com/2020/11/25/reckoning-with-revolution-in-nicaragua/)
- **NACLA Editorial Board**, "On the Coup in Bolivia" (2019). The 2019 removal of Morales named as a coup, the MAS government's own failures named too. Free: [portside.org](https://portside.org/2019-11-16/nacla-coup-bolivia-against-revanchist-violence-and-state-repression)

### Africa

- **Thomas Sankara** (1949 to 1987), _Thomas Sankara Speaks_ (1988). The Burkinabe revolution in his own voice, to his assassination. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/thomassankaraspe00sankrich)
- **Julius Nyerere** (1922 to 1999), _The Arusha Declaration_ (1967). The founding program of Tanzanian Ujamaa, the vision as stated. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/subject/africa/nyerere/1967/arusha-declaration.htm)
- **Sojourner Truth Organization**, _The Fall of Kwame Nkrumah_. Ghana under Nkrumah and his 1966 overthrow, sober about the one-party state's own limits. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/history/usa/pubs/sojournertruth/nkrumah.html)

### The Caribbean

- **Village Voice News (Grenada)**, "Maurice Bishop's Execution" (2025). The 1983 Coard-Bishop split and the executions that killed the revolution from within, days before the US invasion. Free: [villagevoicenews.com](https://villagevoicenews.com/2025/10/19/maurice-bishops-execution-the-day-the-grenada-revolution-died/)
