_What frameworks help us name what we are seeing? What concepts and analyses illuminate the structure beneath the surface?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Theoretical|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes.

When socialism met national liberation in the Global South, it had to rethink itself. Marxism had been written for industrial Europe; here it faced peasant majorities, colonial and neocolonial domination, and questions of culture, race, and nation that the classics barely addressed. This lens gathers the frameworks that resulted: Che's theory of guerrilla struggle, the African socialisms of Nkrumah, Cabral, and Nyerere, and the "twenty-first-century socialism" of the pink tide and the argument it provoked. It builds directly on the analysis of imperialism and dependency in [[03-themes/imperialism-and-the-global-system/index|Imperialism and the Global System]], and it carries the planning and transition debates of [[03-themes/revolution-in-power/index|Revolution in Power]] into the periphery.

## Anchor questions

- Can a small armed nucleus make a revolution, or must the conditions be there first? What did foco theory get right and wrong?
- What is "African socialism," and is it a distinct thing or a name for many different projects?
- Cabral said the liberation movement must "return to the source" of popular culture. What does that mean in practice?
- Is "twenty-first-century socialism" a real alternative, or reformism with a red flag? Who is right, Dieterich or his critics?
- Does public or state ownership get you socialism? What did these theorists say it also requires?

## Materials

All free unless marked otherwise. Each source's standpoint is named.

### Guerrilla strategy and the internationalist horizon

- **Che Guevara** (1928 to 1967), _Guerrilla Warfare: A Method_ (1963). The concentrated statement of foco theory, the strategy that shaped a generation of insurgencies for better and worse. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/guevara/1963/09/guerrilla-warfare.htm)
- **Che Guevara**, _Message to the Tricontinental_ (1967). The call for "two, three, many Vietnams," the internationalist horizon of the moment. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/guevara/1967/04/16.htm)

### African socialism

- **Kwame Nkrumah** (1909 to 1972), _Consciencism_ (1964). An African materialist philosophy folding communalism into a socialist synthesis. The most systematic statement of African socialism as theory. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/consciencism0000kwam)
- **Kwame Nkrumah**, _Neo-Colonialism_ (1965). Independence without economic sovereignty as a new form of control, the book that named neocolonialism. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/subject/africa/nkrumah/neo-colonialism/)
- **Amilcar Cabral** (1924 to 1973), _Return to the Source_ (1973). On culture, class, and the militant's return to popular life. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/returntosourcese0000cabr)
- **Amilcar Cabral**, _The Weapon of Theory_ (1966). Class and the productive forces rethought from the standpoint of the colonized. A cornerstone of Global South Marxism. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/subject/africa/cabral/1966/weapon-theory.htm)
- **Walter Rodney** (1942 to 1980), _Tanzanian Ujamaa and Scientific Socialism_ (1972). A sympathetic Marxist reading of Nyerere's Ujamaa as genuine scientific socialism. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/subject/africa/rodney-walter/works/ujamaaandscientificsocialism.htm)

### Twenty-first-century socialism and its critics

- **Alvaro Garcia Linera** (b. 1962), _The Revolutionary State_ (2013). Bolivia's Marxist vice-president on plurinationalism and why state property alone is not socialism. Free: [criticallegalthinking.com](https://criticallegalthinking.com/2013/06/24/the-revolutionary-state-an-interview-with-alvaro-garcia-linera/)
- **Heinz Dieterich** (b. 1943), _The Socialism of the 21st Century_ (2007). The thesis that gave the pink tide its slogan, in its own voice. Free: [transversal.at](https://transversal.at/transversal/0805/dieterich/en)
- **Alan Woods** (b. 1944), _Reformism or Revolution_ (2008). The Trotskyist rebuttal: Dieterich's model leaves capitalism and the bourgeois state intact. The critic. Free: [marxist.com](https://marxist.com/reformism-or-revolution.htm)
- **Marta Harnecker** (1937 to 2019), _Ideas for the Struggle_ (2010). A reconstruction of movement-based democratic socialism for the Latin American left. A sympathetic architect of the pink tide. Free PDF: [socialist-alliance.org](https://socialist-alliance.org/sites/default/files/ideas_for_the_struggle.pdf)
