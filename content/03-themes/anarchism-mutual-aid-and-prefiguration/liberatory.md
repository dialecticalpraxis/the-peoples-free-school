_What would it mean to overcome or transform this? What have movements imagined, attempted, and built?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Liberatory|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes. The school does not resolve the disagreements among traditions. It puts them in front of the room and lets the room think.

This lens asks the largest question the school poses: what would a free society actually look like, and how would it hold together? Kropotkin, Bookchin, and the Zapatistas answer with communes, assemblies, and autonomy; Le Guin answers with a novel that refuses to pretend the answer is easy. And anarchists argue among themselves about how to get there, whether through disciplined organization, a broad synthesis of tendencies, or insurrection without organization at all. Those internal arguments are part of the curriculum.

## Anchor questions

- What would a free society actually look like, day to day?
- If not the state, who keeps the peace, builds the roads, cares for the vulnerable?
- Communes, councils, cooperatives, federations: how might people coordinate at scale?
- What do the Zapatistas and Rojava show is possible, and what are their limits?
- Anarchists disagree among themselves. What are the real arguments?

## Materials

Free unless marked otherwise. This lens deliberately gathers positions that disagree with each other.

### Visions of a free society

- **Peter Kropotkin** (1842 to 1921), _The Conquest of Bread_ (1892). The classic positive vision of anarchist communism, showing how a society could meet everyone's needs through voluntary common ownership and distribution according to need. Warm, concrete, and shared with [[03-themes/how-capitalism-works/liberatory|the capitalism theme]]. Free: [theanarchistlibrary.org](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/petr-kropotkin-the-conquest-of-bread)
- **Murray Bookchin** (1921 to 2006), _The Next Revolution_ (2015). Bookchin's mature statement of communalism and libertarian municipalism, building a free society through confederated citizen assemblies. The theory behind Rojava, with a foreword by Ursula K. Le Guin. Free: [theanarchistlibrary.org](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/murray-bookchin-next-revolution)
- **Murray Bookchin**, _Libertarian Municipalism: An Overview_ (1991). A short, focused summary of the strategy of neighborhood assemblies and confederal councils, for a group that wants the argument without the full book. Free: [theanarchistlibrary.org](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/murray-bookchin-libertarian-municipalism-an-overview)
- **EZLN (Zapatista Army of National Liberation)**, _Sixth Declaration of the Selva Lacandona_ (2005). The Zapatistas' plainspoken statement of who they are and how they govern themselves from below. A contemporary vision of autonomy grounded in thirty years of practice. Free: [enlacezapatista.ezln.org.mx](https://enlacezapatista.ezln.org.mx/sdsl-en/)
- **Ursula K. Le Guin** (1929 to 2018), _The Dispossessed: An Ambiguous Utopia_ (1974). A novel imagining a functioning anarchist world, Anarres, with honesty about its tensions and failures. The best single prompt for discussing what a free society would feel like to live in. Not free; [author page](https://www.ursulakleguin.com/dispossessed).

### The strategic debates within anarchism

The school holds the disagreements open, and anarchism has its own sharp internal ones about organization.

- **Errico Malatesta** (1853 to 1932), _An Anarchist Programme_ (1920). A concrete organizational statement of anarchist aims and methods, representing the patient, organized, class-struggle current. Shared with [[03-themes/the-state-strategy-and-power/liberatory|The State, Strategy, and Power]]. Free: [theanarchistlibrary.org](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/errico-malatesta-an-anarchist-programme)
- **Dielo Truda group**, _Organisational Platform of the Libertarian Communists_ (1926). The exiled Russian anarchists' call for a united, disciplined anarchist organization, the founding text of "platformism." Free: [theanarchistlibrary.org](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/dielo-truda-workers-cause-organisational-platform-of-the-libertarian-communists)
- **Voline** (1882 to 1945), _The Anarchist Synthesis_ (1934). The counter-argument to platformism, proposing that anarchist communists, syndicalists, and individualists coexist in one broad federation. Read the two together. Free: [theanarchistlibrary.org](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/voline-synthesis-anarchist)
- **CrimethInc.**, _From Democracy to Freedom_ (2016). An insurrectionary, anti-organizational argument that freedom is not any form of democratic rule but direct self-determination. A sharp contrast with the organized currents above, and a good text to argue over. Free: [theanarchistlibrary.org](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/crimethinc-from-democracy-to-freedom)
