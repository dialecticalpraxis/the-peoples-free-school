_What does this look like on the ground, and what are people doing about it now?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Practical|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes.

Prefiguration means you do not wait. This lens is about what people are building right now, without permission: mutual-aid networks, food distributions, cooperatives, tenant unions, disaster-response crews, and the everyday practice of taking care of each other. It is also, unusually, a lens with real how-to material, because the hardest part of prefigurative work is not the idea but the doing: making decisions without a boss, handling money and conflict, and keeping a project alive past the first burst of energy.

## Anchor questions

- What mutual-aid or cooperative projects exist near you? How do they run?
- How do you actually make decisions in a group without a boss? What is consensus?
- What does it take to start a mutual-aid project and keep it alive?
- Why do so many collectives burn out or fall apart, and how do some last?
- What can people build right now, without permission?

## Materials

Free unless marked otherwise.

### Journalism and reporting

- **It's Going Down** (2015 to present). An anarchist and anti-authoritarian news and analysis hub reporting on mutual aid, tenant organizing, and community defense across North America. Free: [itsgoingdown.org](https://itsgoingdown.org/)
- **CrimethInc.** (1996 to present). A decentralized anarchist publishing collective offering analysis, organizing accounts, and free downloadable zines and posters. Free: [crimethinc.com](https://crimethinc.com/)
- **Grassroots Economic Organizing (GEO)** (1991 to present). A grassroots-journalism collective covering worker cooperatives, housing co-ops, and the solidarity economy, with reports and organizing resources. Free: [geo.coop](https://geo.coop/about)

### Practical handbooks and how-to material

- **A Consensus Handbook** (Seeds for Change, 2013). A thorough anti-copyright guide to cooperative decision making and facilitation for co-ops, communities, and activist groups. The single most directly usable how-to for running a horizontal meeting, and ideal to work through as a session. Free: [seedsforchange.org.uk](https://www.seedsforchange.org.uk/handbook)
- **Mutual Aid 101 Toolkit** (Mutual Aid Disaster Relief, 2020). A short, sharable toolkit on starting a neighborhood mutual-aid pod, community agreements, and disaster response, drawn from over thirty communities. Free: [mutualaiddisasterrelief.org](https://mutualaiddisasterrelief.org/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/NO-LOGOS-Mutual-Aid-101_-Toolkit.pdf)
- **Hungry for Peace** (Keith McHenry, 2012). The de facto how-to manual of Food Not Bombs, covering food collection, consensus decision making, logistics, and what to do when police arrive. Free: [foodnotbombs.net](https://foodnotbombs.net/new_site/hungry_for_peace.php)
- **Mutual Aid: Building Solidarity During This Crisis (and the Next)** (Dean Spade, 2020). Part framework, part organizing manual, distinguishing mutual aid from charity and addressing decision making, conflict, burnout, and co-optation. Free full text: [theanarchistlibrary.org](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/dean-spade-mutual-aid)

### Films and documentaries

- **No Gods No Masters: A History of Anarchism** (Tancrède Ramonet, 2016, three parts). A multi-part narrated history tracing anarchism from Proudhon through the Spanish Revolution of 1936. Useful background on the tradition and its living experiments, free with English subtitles. Free: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/NoGodsNoMasters2)
- **The Take** (Avi Lewis and Naomi Klein, 2004, 87 min). Follows laid-off Buenos Aires auto workers who occupy their shuttered plant and run it as a cooperative under the slogan "Occupy, Resist, Produce." A living worker-recuperation experiment. Paywalled: [cinemapolitica.org](https://www.cinemapolitica.org/film/the-take/).

### Podcasts and interviews with organizers

- **The Response** (Shareable, 2019 to present). A documentary and interview series on disaster collectivism and mutual aid, with organizers describing on-the-ground responses to hurricanes, wildfires, and political crises. Free: [shareable.net](https://www.shareable.net/the-response/)
- **Live Like the World is Dying** (Margaret Killjoy, 2020 to present). Interviews on community preparedness and resilience from an explicitly anarchist standpoint, covering skills, mutual aid, and collective survival. Free: [liveliketheworldisdying.com](https://www.liveliketheworldisdying.com/)
- **The Final Straw Radio** (2010 to present). A weekly anarchist interview show out of Southern Appalachia, part of the Channel Zero network, featuring organizers in liberation struggles worldwide. Free: [channelzeronetwork.com](https://channelzeronetwork.com/)

### Explainers on video

- **"What is Mutual Aid?"** (Andrewism, 2020). Roughly 20 minutes, about 51,000 views. Defines mutual aid and distinguishes it from charity, bridging the practical and theoretical lenses. [youtube.com/watch?v=LlF-ZvRHa2o](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LlF-ZvRHa2o)
- **"How To Build Radical Community"** (Andrewism, 2024). Roughly 27 minutes, about 88,000 views. A practical sketch of rebuilding community and mutual support against social isolation. [youtube.com/watch?v=1-Xl6dhDV3Q](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1-Xl6dhDV3Q)
