_What does this look like on the ground, and what are people doing about it now?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Practical|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes.

The care crisis is not abstract. It is a home-care worker on poverty wages, a parent priced out of childcare, a nurse burning out, a nanny separated from her own children, and it is also the movements building power in response: domestic-worker unions, the reproductive justice movement, and campaigns for a real care economy. This lens brings the theme into the present and asks what it takes to organize work whose site is the home.

## Anchor questions

- Who does the paid care work where you live, and on what terms? Who organizes them?
- What would it take to organize workers whose workplace is someone else's home?
- What does reproductive justice mean beyond the right to an abortion?
- How is the care crisis showing up around you, in childcare, eldercare, and health?
- What are people building to share care rather than privatize it?

## Materials

Free unless marked otherwise. Several of these documentaries stream free on PBS or with a library card.

### Journalism and reporting

- **The 19th** (2020 to present). A nonprofit newsroom reporting on caregiving, the childcare economy, and reproductive justice through a gender-and-race lens. Free to read. Free: [19thnews.org](https://19thnews.org/)
- **Rewire News Group** (2012 to present). A national nonprofit newsroom dedicated to reproductive and sexual health, rights, and justice, with the podcasts _Boom! Lawyered_ and _The Breach_. Free: [rewirenewsgroup.com](https://rewirenewsgroup.com/about-us/)
- **"Capitalism's Crisis of Care"** (Nancy Fraser, Dissent, 2016). An accessible interview framing the care crisis as structural to capitalism, and a free way into Fraser's argument for a group that cannot reach the _New Left Review_ essay. Free: [dissentmagazine.org](https://www.dissentmagazine.org/article/nancy-fraser-interview-capitalism-crisis-of-care/)

### Handbooks and how-to material

- **Domestic Worker Organizing Toolbox** (National Domestic Workers Alliance). A free, open organizing toolkit with eleven sections, from theory of change and base-building to strategic campaigns, political education, and communications. The core practical resource for this lens. Free: [organizing.domesticworkers.org](https://organizing.domesticworkers.org/)
- **Reproductive Justice framework** (SisterSong). The originating collective's plain-language explanation of the three-part reproductive justice framework, health, rights, and justice, and its access-not-choice principle. A clear first reading before a reproductive-justice session. Free: [sistersong.net](https://www.sistersong.net/reproductive-justice)
- **A History of Domestic Work and Worker Organizing** (Guglielmo, Joffroy, and Sierra Becerra, with the NDWA). An interactive timeline and the "We Make History" political-education materials on domestic-worker history. Free: [dwherstories.com](https://www.dwherstories.com/)

### Films and documentaries

- **Caregiving** (Chris Durrance, WETA, 2025, 115 min). A PBS documentary on paid and unpaid caregivers and the history and stakes of care in America. Strong on the invisible care workforce, and streams free. Free: [pbs.org](https://www.pbs.org/)
- **Dignidad: Domestic Workers' Journey for Justice in California** (Independent Lens, KVIE, 2022, 27 min). Follows caregivers, nannies, and house cleaners winning workplace-safety protections during the pandemic. A direct look at domestic-worker organizing, and streams free. Free: [pbs.org](https://www.pbs.org/video/dignidad-domestic-workers-journey-for-justice-in-california-3nj1kv/)
- **Care** (Deirdre Fishel, 2016, 60 min). A documentary on the hidden world of in-home elder care and the low-wage women who do it, arguing that care work is essential work deserving a living wage. A free 35-minute version is offered on the film site. Free: [caredocumentary.com](https://caredocumentary.com/)

### Podcasts and interviews with organizers

- **Death Panel** (2018 to present). A leftist political economy of health and care show. The free Thursday episodes lay out how capitalism separates "care" from need and excludes long-term care and disability support, with transcripts. Free: [deathpanel.net](https://www.deathpanel.net/transcripts/s23-health-and-capital)
- **Upstream, "Post-Capitalist Parenting"** (with Toi Smith, 2025). A documentary-podcast pair on how capitalism shapes parenting and reproductive labor, and on reimagining the family. Session-friendly audio from a podcast the school already draws on. Free: [upstreampodcast.org](https://www.upstreampodcast.org/)

### Explainers on video

- **"The work that makes all other work possible"** (Ai-jen Poo, TED, 2019). Roughly 16 minutes, about 88,000 views. The director of the National Domestic Workers Alliance on organizing nannies, cleaners, and home-care workers. A strong practical anchor. [youtube.com/watch?v=JqcDzptviFw](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JqcDzptviFw)
- **"Explained: Why Women Are Paid Less"** (Netflix, Vox, 2020). Roughly 19 minutes, about 1.5 million views. Ties the pay gap to the motherhood penalty and unpaid care. A mainstream explainer to open a session and then push further. [youtube.com/watch?v=hP8dLUxBfsU](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hP8dLUxBfsU)
- **"Loretta Ross Defines Reproductive Justice"** (WebsEdge Medicine, 2022). Roughly 5 minutes, about 10,000 views, a niche pick. A co-founder of the framework defines its three core rights. A short anchor for a reproductive-justice session. [youtube.com/watch?v=lsOKBLmvqGI](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lsOKBLmvqGI)
