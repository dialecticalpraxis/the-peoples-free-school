_What would it mean to overcome or transform this? What have movements imagined, attempted, and built?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Liberatory|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes. The school does not resolve the disagreements among traditions. It puts them in front of the room and lets the room think.

If the private household is where so much unpaid and gendered work is trapped, what would it mean to free it? The traditions here answer differently, and on purpose. Some want to communalize the household's work while keeping its bonds. Some want to socialize care through public provision. Some want to abolish the family as a unit of privatized care altogether, and some want to end the gendered and biological family as such. Gay and trans liberation currents ask what selves and relationships become possible when the old forms loosen.

## Anchor questions

- What would it mean to abolish the family? Abolish what, exactly, and build what instead?
- Can the household be transformed, or must it be undone?
- What have revolutions actually tried, in collective childcare, communal kitchens, new forms of love?
- Wages for housework or the end of gendered work: which points toward freedom?
- What would a world look like where care was everyone's work and no one's cage?

## Materials

Free unless marked otherwise. This lens deliberately gathers positions that disagree with each other.

### Communizing and socializing the household

- **Alexandra Kollontai** (1872 to 1952), _Communism and the Family_ (1920). A direct vision of dissolving the household's economic functions into communal kitchens, laundries, and collective childcare, turning marriage into a free union of affection rather than an economic unit. Written from inside a revolution that tried it. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/kollonta/1920/communism-family.htm)
- **Alexandra Kollontai**, _Make Way for Winged Eros_ (1923). Kollontai's argument that love itself is a social and historical force, proposing "love-comradeship" as a proletarian alternative to possessive, property-based romance. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/kollonta/1923/winged-eros.htm)
- **Angela Y. Davis** (b. 1944), _The Approaching Obsolescence of Housework_ (1981). Davis's influential dissent from Wages for Housework, arguing that paying for housework entrenches it, and that liberation lies in industrializing and socializing domestic labor. Read directly against the Wages for Housework texts in the theoretical lens. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/subject/women/authors/davis-angela/housework.htm)

### Gay and trans liberation

- **Carl Wittman** (1943 to 1986), _Refugees from Amerika: A Gay Manifesto_ (1970). An early gay liberation manifesto linking sexual freedom to the critique of gender roles, the family, and capitalism, and calling for coalition across liberation movements. Free: [historyisaweapon.com](https://www.historyisaweapon.com/defcon1/wittmanmanifesto.html)
- **Gay Liberation Front**, _Gay Liberation Front Manifesto_ (London, 1971). A collective statement rooting gay oppression in the patriarchal nuclear family and rigid gender roles, and imagining new communal ways of living beyond them. Free: [sourcebooks.web.fordham.edu](https://sourcebooks.web.fordham.edu/pwh/glf-london.asp)

### Family abolition

- **Shulamith Firestone** (1945 to 2012), _The Dialectic of Sex_ (1970). The radical feminist classic that locates women's oppression in biological reproduction itself and calls for its technological transformation and the abolition of the biological family. Bold, contested, and generative. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/dialecticofsexth00fire)
- **M.E. O'Brien**, _To Abolish the Family_ (2019). A historical and strategic account of the family abolition tradition from Marx and Engels to the present, arguing that the family privatizes care and that its abolition means universalizing care, not ending relationships. The clearest free entry to the debate. Free: [endnotes.org.uk](https://www.endnotes.org.uk/issues/issue-5/to-abolish-the-family---me-obrien)
- **Sophie Lewis**, _Abolish the Family: A Manifesto for Care and Liberation_ (2022). A short contemporary case for family abolition as the expansion of care and kinship beyond the private household, tracing the demand from Fourier and Kollontai to today. Not free; [publisher page](https://www.versobooks.com/products/2890-abolish-the-family).
- **Sophie Lewis**, _Full Surrogacy Now_ (2019). Uses gestational surrogacy to argue for collective, non-biological models of reproduction and kinship against the privatized family. Not free; [publisher page](https://www.versobooks.com/products/711-full-surrogacy-now).

### Debates and disagreements

The school holds the disagreements open. This lens stages one of the sharpest arguments in socialist feminism.

- Dalla Costa, James, and Federici (in the theoretical lens) argue for wages for housework as a way to make reproductive labor visible and refusable. Angela Davis argues the opposite, that wages entrench housework and the answer is to socialize it. Kollontai wants to communalize the household's work while keeping love and bonds. Firestone, O'Brien, and Lewis want to abolish the family form itself. Put these side by side: they agree the private gendered household must go, and disagree completely about what replaces it.
