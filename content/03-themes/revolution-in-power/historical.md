_How did this come to be? What were the conditions, struggles, and contingencies that produced the current arrangement?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Historical|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes.

Every socialist state was a revolution before it was a regime, and every one of them changed under the pressures of civil war, invasion, blockade, and scarcity. This lens traces those histories across the Eastern and Asian socialist world, the Russian and Chinese revolutions, the Vietnamese and Korean states, the annihilated Indonesian party, the Eastern Bloc and its revolts, and the collapse of 1989 to 1991, from more than one vantage point, so the room can ask what was chosen, what was forced, and what might have gone otherwise. It picks up from the movement history in [[03-themes/origins-dialectics-marx-and-the-workers-movement/index|Origins]] at the moment revolution becomes government, and it builds on the national-liberation histories in [[03-themes/imperialism-and-the-global-system/index|Imperialism and the Global System]].

## Anchor questions

- How did a revolution meant to free workers end with a secret police? Was that inevitable?
- What did the USSR and China actually achieve, and at what cost?
- Why did the Soviet Union and the Eastern Bloc collapse, and who gained from how it ended?
- The largest Communist party outside the ruling states was destroyed in Indonesia in 1965. Why is that history so little known?
- Whose account of these histories do we inherit, and who wrote it?

## Materials

Free unless marked otherwise. "Free to borrow" means a free Internet Archive account and a renewable loan. The sources here are chosen to disagree with each other; the viewpoint of each is noted.

### The Russian Revolution and the USSR

- **John Reed** (1887 to 1920), _Ten Days That Shook the World_ (1919). Eyewitness reporting on October by a communist who was there. The founding pro-Bolshevik narrative. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/reed/1919/10days/10days/)
- **Sheila Fitzpatrick** (b. 1941), _The Russian Revolution_ (1982). The best single overview, from a leading revisionist social historian. Neither apologist nor cold-warrior. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/isbn_2900198806706)
- **Isaac Deutscher** (1907 to 1967), selected writings. Sympathetic to the revolution, critical of its bureaucratic degeneration. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/deutscher/index.htm)
- **Moshe Lewin** (1921 to 2010), _The Soviet Century_ (2005). The system as bureaucratic absolutism, strong on the gulf between state and society. Not free; [publisher page](https://www.versobooks.com/products/1929-the-soviet-century).

### The Chinese Revolution

- **William Hinton** (1919 to 2004), _Fanshen_ (1966). Land reform in one village, the revolution from below. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/fanshendocumentahintrich)
- **Mobo Gao** (b. 1952), _The Battle for China's Past_ (2008). A defense of the Mao era's gains against the standard catastrophe narrative. Read against the Great Leap documentary below. Free PDF: [socialiststories.com](http://www.socialiststories.com/en/writers/Gao-Mobo/The-Battle-for-Chinas-Past-Mobo-Gao.pdf)

### Vietnam, Korea, and Indonesia

- **Ngo Van** (1913 to 2005), _Revolution and Counter-Revolution Under Colonial Rule_ (1995). The anti-Stalinist left's history of the Vietnamese revolution's suppressed rivals. Free: [theanarchistlibrary.org](https://theanarchistlibrary.org/library/ngo-van-xuyet-revolution-and-counterrevolution-under-colonial-rule)
- **Gabriel Kolko** (1932 to 2014), _Anatomy of a War_ (1985). A Marxist structural history from the rise of Vietnamese communism to the fall of Saigon. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/anatomyofwarviet0000kolk)
- **Bruce Cumings** (b. 1943), _North Korea: Another Country_ (2004). The DPRK set against the Korean War and US policy, contextualizing but not credulous. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/northkoreaanothe0000cumi)
- **John Roosa**, _Pretext for Mass Murder_ (2006). The definitive reconstruction of Suharto's 1965 coup and the pretext for destroying the PKI. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/pretextformassmu0000roos)
- **Vincent Bevins**, _The Jakarta Method_ (2020). The PKI's annihilation inside a US-backed global anticommunist program. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/the-jakarta-method-washingtons-anticommunist-crusade-and-the-mass-murder-program_2021)

### The Eastern Bloc and the fall

- **Chris Harman** (1942 to 2009), _Class Struggles in Eastern Europe, 1945 to 1983_ (1988). The workers' revolts, 1953, 1956, 1968, Poland, as risings against the system that ruled in their name. Trotskyist. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/classstrugglesin0000harm)
- **Colin Barker** (1939 to 2019), _Festival of the Oppressed_ (1986). Solidarity as a ten-million-strong workers' movement between reform and revolution. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/festivalofoppres0000bark)
- **Chris Harman**, _The Storm Breaks_ (1990). A left analysis of 1989 written as it happened. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/harman/1990/xx/stormbreaks.html)
