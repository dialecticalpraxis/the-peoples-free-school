_What frameworks help us name what we are seeing? What concepts and analyses illuminate the structure beneath the surface?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Theoretical|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes.

Once the room has the histories and the testimony, the hard question arrives: what _were_ these societies? A workers' state, or a new class dictatorship? Socialism, or state capitalism, or something without a name yet? And could a planned economy have worked, or was the whole project doomed at the level of economics? This lens gathers the rival frameworks, Marxist and anti-Marxist, defender and critic, and sets them arguing. It draws on the theory of the state in [[03-themes/the-state-strategy-and-power/index|The State, Strategy, and Power]] and the planning debates in [[03-themes/how-capitalism-works/index|How Capitalism Works]], and refuses to hand the room a settled answer.

## Anchor questions

- Was the Soviet Union socialist, a "degenerated workers' state," state capitalism, or a new class society? Does the label matter?
- Can an economy be planned without markets? What did Mises say could not be done, and did the socialist states prove or disprove him?
- Does public ownership without workers' control get you anything Marx would have called socialism?
- Is a one-party state a betrayal of the revolution or a condition of its survival?
- If detailed democratic planning is now computationally feasible, what changes?

## Materials

All free unless marked otherwise. Each source's standpoint is named; they are meant to be read against one another.

### What kind of society was it?

- **V.I. Lenin** (1870 to 1924), _The State and Revolution_ (1917). The blueprint the Bolsheviks carried into power: smash the old state, replace it with the armed people, let the state wither. Read against what the Soviet state actually became. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/lenin/works/1917/staterev/index.htm)
- **Leon Trotsky** (1879 to 1940), _The Revolution Betrayed_ (1936). The classic thesis: the USSR was a degenerated workers' state, property nationalized but power usurped by a privileged bureaucratic caste. The founding Trotskyist analysis. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/trotsky/1936/revbet/)
- **Tony Cliff** (1917 to 2000), _State Capitalism in Russia_ (1948). The main rival on the left: not a workers' state at all but bureaucratic state capitalism, the bureaucracy acting as a collective capitalist under the pressure of arms competition. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/cliff/works/1955/statecap/index.htm)
- **Milovan Djilas** (1911 to 1995), _The New Class_ (1957). A former Yugoslav vice-president, writing from prison, argues the party-bureaucracy became a new exploiting class that owned the state. Free to borrow: [archive.org](https://archive.org/details/newclassanalysis0000djil)

### The calculation and planning debate

- **Ludwig von Mises** (1881 to 1973), _Economic Calculation in the Socialist Commonwealth_ (1920). The founding anti-socialist argument, and the challenge every planning proposal must answer: without market prices for capital goods, rational allocation is impossible. Austrian-school and hostile. Free: [mises.org](https://mises.org/library/economic-calculation-socialist-commonwealth)
- **Paul Sweezy and Charles Bettelheim**, _On the Transition to Socialism_ (1971). A sympathetic but searching exchange on why transitional economies drift back toward markets and commodity production. Free PDF: [marx2mao.com](http://www.marx2mao.com/PDFs/OTTS71.pdf)
- **Alec Nove** (1915 to 1994), _The Economics of Feasible Socialism_ (1983). A market-socialist's case that central planning cannot process the information a modern economy generates, with a sketch of a mixed alternative. Free PDF: [digamo.free.fr](http://digamo.free.fr/nove91.pdf)
- **W. Paul Cockshott and Allin Cottrell**, _Towards a New Socialism_ (1993). The reply from the other side: modern computation makes detailed democratic planning feasible, using labor-time accounting. The strongest recent case for planning. Free PDF: [wfu.edu](https://users.wfu.edu/cottrell/socialism_book/new_socialism.pdf)
- **David Schweickart** (b. 1942), _Economic Democracy_ (2011). A model of market socialism built on worker self-managed firms and social control of investment, learning from the command economy without abandoning planned investment. The book is not free; this essay states the case. Free essay: [thenextsystem.org](https://thenextsystem.org/sites/default/files/2017-08/NewSystems_DavidSchweickart.pdf)

### Video

- **"The Soviet Economy, Explained"** (Asianometry, 2020). Roughly 18 minutes, about 553,000 views. A measured, critical walk through Gosplan, plan-fulfillment incentives, and chronic shortage. [youtube.com/watch?v=geKQnRx3-cc](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=geKQnRx3-cc)
- **"Explaining the Economy of the Soviet Union"** (Hakim, 2022). Roughly 28 minutes, about 397,000 views. A Marxist-Leninist defense foregrounding growth, full employment, and social provision. The deliberate counterweight to the video above. [youtube.com/watch?v=nGm0u3UHDZM](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nGm0u3UHDZM)
