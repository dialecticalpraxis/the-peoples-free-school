_What does this look like on the ground, and what are people doing about it now?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Practical|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes.

This lens holds two things: **the food system, where the theme's question gets asked three times a day**, and **the organizing, where people who were answered built the thing they had been refused**.

The order matters. The food material makes the argument; the Panthers and the Freedom Farms close it. If the counter-histories come first they read as uplift and let the argument off the hook. They belong last, and framed exactly one way: **they fed themselves and treated themselves because the state had already told them what their bodies were worth.**

## Anchor questions

- Karen Washington says a food desert is an outsider's word for a place that is full of life. What does the word desert do, and who does it do it for?
- What conditions are attached to being fed in this country? What are the conditions for?
- Crop workers die of heat at roughly twenty times the rate of other workers. Is that a tragedy or a price?
- If the cheapest calories are engineered to be cheap and then aimed at your children, whose fault is your body?
- ACT UP made the FDA move. What did they have that made that possible, and can it be done again?
- The Panthers ran free clinics and the first mass sickle cell screening in the country. What does it mean that they had to?

### Cheap calories, aimed

**Assign these two together or neither.** The first on its own is a story about corn subsidies that would read identically if every affected body were white; it becomes part of this theme only when the second one shows where the calories were pointed.

- **Do and colleagues** (2020). Eating from the subsidized commodity stream tracks with cardiometabolic risk, at population scale. Free: [pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7690710/)

## A note on method

**Put the counter-histories last and frame them.** The Panthers and the Freedom Farms are the most cheering material in this theme, and that is the danger: a room can leave feeling that communities are resilient and forget that resilience was the bill for a decision someone made. They fed and treated themselves *because* the question had already been answered. Framed that way they close the argument. Framed as inspiration they replace it.

**ACT UP is the hardest case in the theme, and worth the argument.** It is the great victory: a movement of sick people who learned the science, forced the FDA, and got the drugs. It is also the case that most tempts a room into thinking the system can be moved by sufficiently brilliant organizing. Ask what ACT UP had that a fat Black woman in an examining room does not.
