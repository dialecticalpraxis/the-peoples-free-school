_What would freedom actually require here? What are people already doing to build it, and what remains unresolved?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Liberatory|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes.

At the birth of the movement lies an argument it never finished, and the school does not pretend to finish it either: does the road to freedom run through reform or revolution? Around 1900 the German party staged this debate at its sharpest, Bernstein arguing that a mellowing capitalism made revolution unnecessary, Kautsky holding an orthodox center, and Luxemburg insisting that to give up revolution is to give up socialism. It is not a museum piece. Every socialist and every organizer since has had to place themselves in it, and this lens keeps it open rather than declaring a winner. The same argument, carried into the era of state power, runs through [[03-themes/the-state-strategy-and-power/index|The State, Strategy, and Power]]; here we meet it at its source.

## Anchor questions

- Reform or revolution: is that still the real choice, or a false one? Can you have one without the other?
- Bernstein said capitalism was adapting and softening. Was he wrong, or early?
- Luxemburg trusted the spontaneous self-activity of workers over party machinery. Where have you seen that trust rewarded, or betrayed?
- Kautsky counseled patience, build and wait. When is patience wisdom and when is it an excuse?
- If you had been in that party in 1905, whose side would you have taken, and why?

## Materials

All free. This is one argument in several voices; read them against each other.

### The reform pole

- **Eduard Bernstein** (1850 to 1932), _Evolutionary Socialism_ (1899). The founding case for the gradual, parliamentary road: capitalism is adapting, so socialism arrives by reform. Revisionist, argued in good faith. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/bernstein/works/1899/evsoc/)

### The orthodox center

- **Karl Kautsky** (1854 to 1938), _The Class Struggle_ (1892). The canonical exposition of pre-revisionist Marxism and the baseline the debate departs from. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/kautsky/1892/erfurt/index.htm)
- **Karl Kautsky**, _The Road to Power_ (1909). The strategy of patient attrition, build, win majorities, wait. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/kautsky/1909/power/index.htm)

### The revolutionary pole

- **Rosa Luxemburg** (1871 to 1919), _Reform or Revolution_ (1900). Her direct reply to Bernstein: reforms are means, not the end, and to renounce revolution is to renounce socialism. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/luxemburg/1900/reform-revolution/index.htm)
- **Rosa Luxemburg**, _The Mass Strike_ (1906). Workers' self-activity as the living form of revolution, outrunning cautious leaders. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/luxemburg/1906/mass-strike/index.htm)
- **Rosa Luxemburg**, _Theory and Practice_ (1910). Her break with Kautsky, the moment the revolutionary left split from the orthodox center. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/luxemburg/1910/theory-practice/index.htm)

## Debates and disagreements

This lens is a debate by design. Some threads to hold open rather than resolve:

- **Reform or revolution, or reform _and_ revolution?** Luxemburg did not oppose fighting for reforms; she opposed mistaking them for the destination. Is there a coherent position that uses reforms to build revolutionary capacity, or does that collapse in practice toward one pole?
- **Was Bernstein refuted or vindicated?** The welfare states of the twentieth century look like his prediction coming true; their limits and rollback look like Luxemburg's warning. The room can argue which history we are living in.
- **Spontaneity and organization.** Luxemburg trusted the mass strike and the self-activity of workers; Kautsky trusted the disciplined party. This tension runs forward into every later debate about the party, and connects to the anarchist critique in [[03-themes/anarchism-mutual-aid-and-prefiguration/index|Anarchism, Mutual Aid, and Prefiguration]].
