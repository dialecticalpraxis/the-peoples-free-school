_What frameworks help us name what we are seeing? What concepts and analyses illuminate the structure beneath the surface?_

See [[02-pedagogy#Theoretical|the pedagogy document]] for what this lens does across all themes.

Marxism began as a philosophy before it was an economics or a politics, and this lens follows that formation: Hegel's dialectic, in which history moves by contradiction toward the realization of freedom; Feuerbach's materialist turn against religion; and the young Marx's break from both, arriving at a method that grounds ideas in material life and practice. These are the founding moves of dialectical and historical materialism. The _economics_ that grew from this method lives in [[03-themes/how-capitalism-works/index|How Capitalism Works]], and the _theory of the state and strategy_ in [[03-themes/the-state-strategy-and-power/index|The State, Strategy, and Power]]; this lens is where the method itself is forged.

## Anchor questions

- What is a dialectic? What does it mean to say history moves by contradiction?
- Marx said he turned Hegel right side up. What did he keep, and what did he invert?
- What is "materialism" here, and what does it deny? Does it leave room for ideas to matter?
- What is alienation, and does the young Marx's account still describe your working life?
- "The philosophers have only interpreted the world; the point is to change it." Is that a theory, a slogan, or both?

## Materials

All free. The primary texts are short; a session can read one aloud together.

### The dialectic and its materialist turn

- **G. W. F. Hegel** (1770 to 1831), _Introduction to the Philosophy of History_ (1837). History as the dialectical self-development of freedom, the idealist dialectic Marx would invert. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/hegel/works/hi/introduction.htm)
- **Ludwig Feuerbach** (1804 to 1872), _The Essence of Christianity_ (1841). The Left-Hegelian argument that God is human nature projected outward, the hinge to materialism. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/reference/archive/feuerbach/works/essence/index.htm)

### The young Marx forms the method

- **Karl Marx**, _Critique of Hegel's Philosophy of Right, Introduction_ (1844). Philosophy meets its agent, the proletariat; the "opium of the people" passage in context. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1843/critique-hpr/intro.htm)
- **Karl Marx**, _Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts of 1844_. Alienated labor and human self-realization, the formation of Marx's humanism. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1844/manuscripts/preface.htm)
- **Karl Marx**, _Theses on Feuerbach_ (1845). Eleven terse notes, the seed crystal of historical materialism. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1845/theses/index.htm)
- **Marx and Engels**, _The German Ideology, Part I_ (1846). The first full statement of the materialist conception of history. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1845/german-ideology/index.htm)

### The movement systematizes the method

- **Friedrich Engels**, _Socialism: Utopian and Scientific_ (1880). The most read short statement of dialectics and historical materialism. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1880/soc-utop/index.htm)
- **Friedrich Engels**, _Ludwig Feuerbach and the End of Classical German Philosophy_ (1886). The tradition's own account of its debt to Hegel and Feuerbach. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/marx/works/1886/ludwig-feuerbach/index.htm)
- **Georgi Plekhanov** (1856 to 1918), _Fundamental Problems of Marxism_ (1907). The influential Second International synthesis into a systematic worldview. Free: [marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/plekhanov/1907/fundamental-problems.htm)
