title: "Critical Pedagogy and the Practice of Liberation"
---

How does education work, and how could it work? This theme is the school studying itself. It covers Freire, Horton, popular education, the folk-school tradition, the politics of literacy, and the long history of people learning together against a world that does not want them to learn. It is the ground the other themes stand on, and it pairs closely with the [[02-pedagogy|Pedagogy document]] and the [[04-facilitator-guide|facilitator guide]] that shape every session.

## How to use this theme

This folder organizes resources by lens. See the [[02-pedagogy|Pedagogy document]] for what the lenses do and why they are structured this way.

- [[03-themes/critical-pedagogy-and-the-practice-of-liberation/experiential|Experiential]]: What is your experience with this? What does the room already know?
- [[03-themes/critical-pedagogy-and-the-practice-of-liberation/historical|Historical]]: How did this come to be?
- [[03-themes/critical-pedagogy-and-the-practice-of-liberation/theoretical|Theoretical]]: How do we understand and analyze it?
- [[03-themes/critical-pedagogy-and-the-practice-of-liberation/practical|Practical]]: What is happening on the ground and what are people doing about it?
- [[03-themes/critical-pedagogy-and-the-practice-of-liberation/liberatory|Liberatory]]: What would it mean to overcome or transform this?

A session does not need to use all five lenses. A study circle may move across them. Use what serves the room. This theme also works well as a first theme for a new group and as a facilitator training, since it makes the school's own method the object of study.

## Anchor questions

These questions have proven useful for opening up this theme. They are starting points, not a script. Take the ones that fit the room and leave the rest.

- When have you learned something that changed how you see the world? How did that happen?
- What was school like for you? What did it teach you about yourself and your place?
- Who have been your real teachers, in or out of any classroom?
- Have you ever been taught as if you were empty, a container to be filled? How did it feel?
- What do you already know that no school gave you?
- What is this room for? What kind of learning are we trying to do here?
- Can education free people, or does it mostly train them to fit? When is it which?

## Recommended sequences

Suggested arcs for groups new to this theme. Adapt freely. Every session opens with a check-in and closes with reflection (see [[02-pedagogy#Reflective practice|the pedagogy document]]). Because this theme is about the room's own practice, notice throughout whether the way you are studying it matches what it argues.

**Single-session entry point (2 hours).** Start experiential: people's own histories as learners, and the teacher who saw them or the classroom that shut them down. Watch a short piece together (Freire in his own voice, [Paulo Freire: An Incredible Conversation](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aFWjnkFypFA)). Read the banking-model passage of _Pedagogy of the Oppressed_ aloud and test it against the room's experience. Close by asking what kind of learning this room is for. A strong first session for any new group. See [[single-session|the single-session template]].

**Six-week study circle.** Week 1 experiential (our own schooling). Week 2 historical: the folk-school and Freedom School lineage, using Horton and the SNCC materials. Weeks 3 to 4 theoretical: Freire's banking model and conscientization, with bell hooks and _We Make the Road by Walking_. Week 5 practical: how to facilitate, using the Highlander methods, Theatre of the Oppressed, and the study-circle guide, ideally by practicing on each other. Week 6 liberatory: the arguments about the method itself, transform schooling versus deschool it, and the feminist, Indigenous, and practitioner critiques of critical pedagogy. See [[study-circle-6week|the six-week template]].

**Weekend intensive.** Compress the arc above into a Friday-evening-through-Sunday structure. This theme makes an excellent facilitator-training weekend, with Saturday on theory and Sunday spent practicing facilitation and Theatre of the Oppressed exercises. A screening of _You Got to Move_ or _Freedom Summer_ works well on Saturday evening. See [[weekend-intensive|the weekend-intensive template]].
